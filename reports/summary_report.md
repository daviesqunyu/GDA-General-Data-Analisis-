# Nairobi Business Intelligence — Summary Report

## Executive Summary

This report summarises insights from the Nairobi Business Analytics project: a simulated e-commerce/retail dataset for a Kenyan business operating in Nairobi (Jan–Dec 2024). The analysis covers revenue trends, customer behaviour, product performance, payment methods, support satisfaction, and sales forecasting.

---

## Key Findings

1. **Revenue seasonality**  
   Revenue peaks in Q4 (November–December), consistent with festive-season demand, and is relatively lower in Q1 (January–February).

2. **Payment methods**  
   M-Pesa dominates transactions, reflecting typical Kenyan payment behaviour. Card and bank transfer contribute a smaller share.

3. **Geographic concentration**  
   Westlands, CBD, Karen, and Kilimani account for a large share of customers and revenue. Delivery and marketing efforts can be prioritised by location.

4. **Customer segments (RFM + K-Means)**  
   Four segments were identified: **Champions** (high value, recent), **Loyal Customers**, **At Risk**, and **Lost**. Tailored campaigns (e.g. loyalty for Champions, win-back for Lost) are recommended.

5. **Product categories**  
   Electronics and Clothing drive the highest revenue; Food and Beauty show strong volume. Category-level pricing and promotions can be optimised using these insights.

6. **Support and satisfaction**  
   Satisfaction scores vary by issue type. Delivery and payment issues should be prioritised to improve retention and reduce churn.

7. **Forecast**  
   A simple linear trend on 2024 monthly revenue suggests continued growth into early 2025; the forecast should be updated as new data becomes available.

---

## Recommendations

1. Run targeted campaigns for **At Risk** and **Lost** segments (e.g. discounts, personalised offers).
2. Reward **Champions** with exclusive offers and early access to promotions.
3. Strengthen delivery and payment processes to reduce support tickets and improve satisfaction.
4. Allocate inventory and marketing budget by category and location using the revenue and customer distribution analyses.
5. Monitor month-over-month revenue and compare with the forecast to detect deviations early.
